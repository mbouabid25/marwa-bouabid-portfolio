Bottom line

Forecast AFCON 2025 live as it happened — and became the foundation for the World Cup engine that hit 67.3%.

Overview

An end-to-end machine learning system that forecasts AFCON 2025 — every match, group table, and knockout bracket, through to the champion. It pairs a Bayesian Poisson goal model with a neural network, runs 5,000 Monte Carlo tournament simulations, and re-forecasts automatically as real results come in.

I'm from Morocco, and Morocco hosted this AFCON — so while I attended matches over winter break, the model was predicting them. It started as an excuse to learn Monte Carlo methods and live data pipelines; it became a full forecasting product.

How it works
  • Team strength ratings — 152 years of international results (1872–2024), weighted by recency with Bayesian priors, produce attack and defence ratings for all 24 teams — plus a host-nation boost for Morocco.
  • Match forecasting — a Poisson goal model converts ratings into win/draw/loss probabilities and most-likely scorelines for every fixture.
  • A deep-learning second opinion — a convolutional neural network trained on team embeddings and recent-form features cross-checks the statistical model.
  • Tournament simulation — 5,000 Monte Carlo runs of the full tournament yield each team's odds of reaching every stage, quarter-finals through champion.
  • Live updates — real results are ingested via a CLI tool; group tables and all downstream forecasts refresh automatically as the tournament progresses.
  • Scenario analysis — baseline vs. three alternative scenarios show how a single tight fixture can reshape the entire knockout bracket.
Results & takeaways

The engine delivers its forecasts through the live demo above — champion odds, group-finish projections, and per-match probability breakdowns — plus exportable Excel reports, all computed from live tournament data.

The project covers the full analytics lifecycle: sourcing and cleaning historical data, engineering features, comparing statistical and deep-learning models, quantifying uncertainty through simulation, and keeping predictions current as new data arrives.
