Overview

A systematic, quantitative equity portfolio construction tool built on modern portfolio theory and ML techniques. Sources constituents from major global indices and applies a "barbell" selection strategy — combining high-momentum and low-volatility equities with configurable geographic diversification.

Approach
  • Four optimization strategies evaluated head-to-head: Hierarchical Risk Parity (HRP), Maximum Sharpe Ratio, Maximum Diversification, and Black-Litterman with investor views.
  • Barbell strategy selects high-momentum stocks paired with low-volatility anchors across S&P 500, FTSE 100, DAX, Nikkei 225, and other indices.
  • Rigorous walk-forward backtesting with realistic transaction cost modelling and survivorship bias awareness — no look-ahead leakage.
  • Comprehensive analytics: factor exposure analysis, risk contribution decomposition, and correlation heatmaps for portfolio transparency.
Results

HRP consistently outperformed on drawdown control while Black-Litterman allowed incorporation of informed macro views. Transaction cost modelling narrowed the apparent alpha of high-turnover strategies — a crucial real-world reality check.
