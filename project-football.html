Bottom line

Turned a $2.3M transfer budget into an optimized 25-player squad — every pick backed by data rather than market hype.

Overview

Football clubs spend hundreds of millions on player transfers based largely on gut feel and hype. This project replaced that speculation with a data-driven framework that identifies undervalued, tactically diverse talent and optimises a 25-player squad within a hard budget constraint.

How it works
  • Player valuation — Random Forest models predict performance from historical data, surfacing statistically undervalued players across global leagues.
  • Tactical profiling — K-Means clustering groups players into tactical archetypes by playing style, not just position labels.
  • Budget optimization — Integer Linear Programming allocates the $2.3M budget across 25 roster spots under positional and tactical constraints.
  • Validation — results checked against market valuations to quantify ROI over a comparable market-rate squad.
