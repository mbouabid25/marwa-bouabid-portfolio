Bottom line

Built a globally optimal 25-man squad for €297M — under 15% of budget — with every signing backed by data rather than market hype.

Overview

Football clubs spend hundreds of millions on transfers based largely on gut feel and hype. For the from-scratch club Durham Top Dogs F.C., this project replaced that speculation with a data-driven pipeline built in R: screening 18,207 players (FIFA 19 dataset, 57 attributes) to find undervalued, tactically diverse talent and assemble a mathematically optimal 25-man squad.

How it works
  • Player valuation — a Random Forest (200 trees) predicts each player's objective market value; the gap between predicted and listed price flags hidden bargains. R² of 96%, with ~2.4× lower RMSE than LASSO or quantile regression.
  • Tactical profiling — K-Means clustering groups players into four archetypes (Defensive Rocks, Creative Playmakers, Pacy Attackers, Solid Goalkeepers) so the squad is stylistically diverse, not just highly rated.
  • Budget optimization — Integer Linear Programming selects the globally optimal 25-man squad under a €2B cap and strict quotas (3 GK / 8 DEF / 8 MID / 6 FWD) — beating a greedy baseline by 17 overall points while committing just €297M.
  • Chemistry lineups — heuristic cohesion scores (+3 shared club, +1 shared nationality) generate three tactical XIs: balanced 4-4-2, defensive 5-3-2, and offensive 4-3-3.
