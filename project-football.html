Overview

Football clubs spend hundreds of millions on player transfers based largely on gut feel and hype. This project replaced that speculation with a data-driven framework that identifies undervalued, tactically diverse talent and optimises a 25-player squad within a hard budget constraint.

Approach
  • Built Random Forest models to predict player performance metrics from historical data, identifying statistically undervalued players across global leagues.
  • Applied K-Means clustering to profile tactical archetypes — grouping players by playing style rather than position labels alone.
  • Formulated an Integer Linear Programming (ILP) optimisation model with constraints on budget ($2.3M), squad size (25), and positional requirements.
  • Validated results against market valuations to quantify ROI over a comparable market-rate squad.
Output

The final deliverable was a "Game Day Playbook" — a management decision-support tool presenting the optimised squad with per-player justification, tactical fit analysis, and projected performance benchmarks.
