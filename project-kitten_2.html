Why this project?

After visiting the Durham Animal Shelter and leaving with three fosters, Marwa saw too many animals in distress. She wanted to build a model that could help shelters prioritise their efforts and reduce the time kittens spend in foster care. This project is the result.

Approach
  • Combined intake metadata (age, breed), medical records, and raw intake photos into a unified prediction pipeline for days-to-adoption.
  • Survival modelling framework estimates adoption hazard curves — producing ranked views and intervention tips for animals least likely to be adopted quickly.
  • Grad-CAM interpretability overlays reveal exactly which visual cues (lighting, expression, framing) push adoption predictions up or down.
  • Simple actionable flags for age, weight, and fixed status turn complex model outputs into a clear to-do list for shelter staff.
Results

The hybrid survival model cut median absolute error by ~2.4 days vs baseline. Grad-CAM confirmed that photo quality (lighting, composition) is a significant adoption predictor — enabling targeted photography recommendations for harder-to-place animals.
